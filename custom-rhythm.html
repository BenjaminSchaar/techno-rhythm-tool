Accent Techniques Explained

ğŸ’¡ Velocity Accents

The classic approach. Accented hits are simply played louder (higher velocity). This is what the TR-909 "accent" button does. Simple, effective, and used on 99% of techno tracks.

ğŸšï¸ Layer Accents

Add a highpass-filtered version of the sound on accented beats. This creates a "crack" or "snap" that cuts through the mix. Common on kicks and snares in modern techno. The filtered layer adds harmonics and presence.

ğŸµ Pitch Accents

Shift the pitch up slightly (~+5%) on accented hits. Works GREAT on claps and hi-hats! Creates a rolling, evolving feel. Less common on kicks. This technique adds movement and groove.

ğŸ”§ In Your DAW

Import your MIDI and try these: 1) Map accents to different samples, 2) Route accent track to a separate channel with effects, 3) Use automation to modulate filter/pitch on accent hits. Get creative!

ğŸ¹ MIDI Note Mapping

Accent Track uses unique notes per drum: Kick accent=35, Snare=40, HH Closed=44, HH Open=49, Clap=82, Perc=54. Map these to custom samples! Main track uses standard notes (Kick=36, Snare=38, etc.). See MIDI-MAPPING-GUIDE.md for full details.
