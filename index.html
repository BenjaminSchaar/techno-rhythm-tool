Understanding Techno Rhythms

Four-on-the-Floor

The foundation of techno: a kick drum on every quarter note (beats 1, 2, 3, 4). This driving rhythm is what makes people move.

Off-Beat Hi-Hats

Closed hi-hats on the off-beats create forward momentum, while open hi-hats on the "&" of beats add energy and release.

Accent & Velocity

Yellow/gold highlighted beats are ACCENTED - they're played louder (velocity 110 vs 80). This creates groove and dynamics, making your rhythm feel alive, not robotic.

Accent Techniques

Accents can be created by: 1) Higher velocity (louder hit), 2) Layering a filtered version (highpass), or 3) Pitch shifting slightly up/down (great for claps/hats). Try the Custom Rhythm editor to experiment!

Clean vs. Accent Track

Download "Clean MIDI" for all hits at equal velocity, or "With Accent Track" to get a separate track showing only accented beats - perfect for layering or creating custom accent sounds in your DAW.

Accent Track MIDI Notes

Each drum has unique accent notes: Kick=35, Snare=40, Closed HH=44, Open HH=49, Clap=82, Perc=54. Map these to custom samples in your DAW - punchy kicks, rimshots, crashes, shakers - get creative!

The TR-909 Legacy

Most patterns here are inspired by the legendary Roland TR-909, the drum machine that defined techno, house, and electronic music.
