## ğŸšï¸ Accent Types Explained

### **ğŸ’¡ Velocity Accents (Standard)**
**What it does:** Accented hits are played **louder**

**When to use:**
- Classic TR-909 style
- Most common in techno
- Simple and effective
- Works on all drums

**Technical:** Accent = velocity 110, Normal = velocity 80

---

### **ğŸšï¸ Layer Accents (Textural)**
**What it does:** Adds a **highpass-filtered click** on accented beats

**When to use:**
- Modern hard techno / industrial
- Add "crack" or "snap" to kicks
- Create presence and cut through the mix
- Want accents to have different tonal character

**How it works:**
- Base sound plays at normal volume
- Additional highpass-filtered noise burst plays simultaneously
- Creates harmonics and brightness

**Pro tip:** In your DAW, use the accent track to trigger a transient shaper or add distortion only on accents!

---

### **ğŸµ Pitch Accents (Groovy)**
**What it does:** Shifts pitch **up 5%** on accented beats

**When to use:**
- Claps (sounds AMAZING!)
- Hi-hats (creates rolling feel)
- Percussion
- Less common on kicks (but try it!)

**Why it works:**
- Creates movement and evolution
- Natural groove feel
- Adds musicality to percussive elements

**Pro tip:** Subtle pitch changes create groove without being obvious. Your brain perceives it as "more interesting" even if you can't consciously hear the pitch shift!

## ğŸ“ Theory: Why Accents Matter

### **Groove = Dynamics**
- Without accents: robotic, flat, lifeless
- With accents: human feel, movement, energy

### **The TR-909 Accent Button**
- Hardware 909 had ONE accent button
- Could only accent ONE instrument at a time
- This limitation created classic grooves!

### **Modern Production**
- You can accent EVERY drum differently
- Layer different accent types
- Create complex polyrhythms
- But remember: **less is more**
